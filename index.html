KlipQ

A Clipboard Manager for macOS

There were often times when I'd wish I could copy multiple things in a row, and paste them in a row. Times when I'd wish I could paste that item I copied four copies ago. Enter: KlipQ

KlipQ is a clipboard manager I made for the simple purpose of saving a history of your recently copied items, and allowing you to paste directly from that history.

My hope is that others get as much use out of this as I do.

Daily Notes Manager

Customize your Obsidian Daily Notes

Obsidian is my must-have tool for notetaking. The Daily Notes feature makes it easy to create a unique note every day. I realized a feature I needed was to have one daily note to include content from other notes, so I created a Daily Notes Manager to help.

I use it to carry over unfinished tasks from the previous day, rollover a "yesterday's work" summary into my "Daily Standup" section, archive old notes, create custom organization based on day of the week, and more.

Door Aura

Custom do-not-disturb indicator display

Door Aura

Do-not-disturb made easy

Working remotely comes with a few challenges, especially when you don't live alone. I wanted a way to indicate my availability without anyone ever having to knock on my door.

The concept is simple; a small LCD outside the door displays a status that I control via IR remote from my desk. I even included an RGB backlight, so color can change depending on the status. Green for "enter," red for "don't." Yellow means I'm on a call.

Door Aura allows for any number of custom presets, each customizable with a variety of settings.

HotKeypad


A macro keypad made from a switch tester

HotKeypad

From Switch Tester to Macro Keypad

As I sometimes do, I was scrolling Amazon, thinking, "what can I make from that?" with each item I saw. Eventually I saw a switch tester, and I had a very interesting idea.

A fun project turned into a practical tool with HotKeypad. I wired a key matrix onto the switches to create a functional keypad, and wrote software to interface with my computer.

Now, with the click of a button, I can perform a variety of tasks and automations on a variety of devices.
